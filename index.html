General Advice: What to Do in Common Cases

Car Accident

If you're involved in a car accident, ensure your safety first, contact emergency services, and collect evidence such as photos, witness statements, and police reports. Do not admit fault at the scene and consult with a lawyer to discuss your legal rights.

Workplace Injury

After a workplace injury, report the incident to your employer, seek immediate medical attention, and document the accident. It's important to file a workers' compensation claim and consult an attorney to ensure your rights are protected.

Medical Malpractice

If you believe you've been a victim of medical malpractice, seek a second opinion immediately and keep a detailed record of all treatments. Collect evidence and consult with an experienced malpractice lawyer to explore your options for pursuing a claim.

How Personal Injury Lawyers Get Paid & Client Compensation Breakdown

Personal injury lawyers typically work on a contingency fee basis, meaning they only get paid if they win the case. Their fee is a percentage of the final settlement or court award, usually ranging between 25% and 40%.

Breakdown of Payment Structure

  • Lawyer's Fee: Typically, 33.3% (or 1/3) of the total compensation is taken by the lawyer. However, this can vary:
    • 25% if settled early (before filing a lawsuit).
    • 33.3% if settled after filing but before trial.
    • 40% if the case goes to trial.
  • Client's Share: The client receives the remaining amount (60% to 75%) after the lawyer’s fee is deducted.
  • The client may also need to cover case expenses (court fees, expert witnesses, etc.), which are sometimes deducted separately.
